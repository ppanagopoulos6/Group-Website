About Morocco

Egypt Kenya South Africa Nigeria Morocco

Morocco is a country where history, culture, and landscape come together in a uniquely captivating way. Located at the crossroads of Africa, Europe, and the Arab world, Morocco has been shaped by Berber civilizations, Arab dynasties, and Mediterranean influences for thousands of years. Its history stretches from ancient Carthaginian trading routes and Roman cities like Volubilis to powerful Islamic dynasties such as the Almoravids and Almohads, who helped shape the architecture and identity of North Africa and Spain.

Top Destinations

1. Marrakech-The Red City

Marrakech is one of Morocco's most iconic cities, known for its intense colors, lively atmosphere, and deep cultural roots. The heart of the city is Jemaa el-Fnaa, a huge square filled with performers, food stalls, musicians, storytellers, and traditional markets. The surrounding souks form a maze of shops selling spices, carpets, lanterns, leather goods, and handmade crafts.

2. Fez

One of the oldest cities in Morocco, famous for its medieval old town (Fez el-Bali), traditional leather tanneries, and rich history.

3. Essaouira-Atlantic Coastal City

Essaouira is a charming port town on the Atlantic Ocean with historic walls, a fishing harbor with blue boats, and a relaxed atmosphere great for walks and seafood.

4. Casablanca

Casablanca is Morocco’s largest city. Its most famous landmark is the Hassan II Mosque, one of the largest and most beautiful in the country, located by the sea.

5.Chefchaouen-The Blue City

Located in the Rif Mountains, Chefchaouen is famous for its dreamy blue-painted old town. Walking through its narrow alleys feels almost magical, as different shades of blue blend with the traditional Moroccan architecture.

Morocco Cuisine

Tagine

A slow-cooked stew made with meat, vegetables, and spices, cooked in a traditional clay pot.

Couscous

Steamed semolina grains served with vegetables and often meat — a classic Moroccan meal.

Harira

A traditional soup made with lentils, chickpeas, tomatoes, and spices, especially popular during Ramadan.
