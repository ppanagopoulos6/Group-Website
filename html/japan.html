JAPAN

About Japan

Japan is a fascinating mix of ancient traditions and modern technology. Visitors can enjoy historic temples, peaceful gardens, futuristic cities, beautiful mountains, and delicious food, all within a very safe and organized country.

Cities to Visit

Tokyo

Tokyo is the most populous metropolitan area in the world. It is Japan’s economic center and a highly modern city known for its shopping districts and global pop culture influence.

Kyoto

Kyoto is considered the cultural capital of Japan. It is home to numerous Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, palaces, and gardens.

Osaka

Osaka is known as “Japan’s kitchen.” It is famous for its lively atmosphere, modern shopping districts, delicious street food, and friendly locals.

Hiroshima

Hiroshima is best known for its message of peace and resilience. Today it is a vibrant city with beautiful rivers and the famous floating torii gate nearby.

Mount Fuji

Mount Fuji is Japan’s highest mountain. This active volcano is famous for its nearly perfect symmetrical cone and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Japanese Food

Sushi and Sashimi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish made with vinegared rice and fresh ingredients like raw fish, seafood, or vegetables.

Ramen

Ramen is a popular noodle soup made with wheat noodles served in a rich broth, often topped with sliced pork and eggs.

Wagyu

Wagyu meat is famous for its rich marbling, tenderness, and flavor, valued in dishes like sukiyaki and steak.

Tips for Travelers

  1. Use public transport – Trains are clean, fast, and easy to use.
  2. Be punctual – Trains and appointments run exactly on time.
  3. Be quiet in public – Especially on trains and buses.
  4. Carry cash – Many small shops don’t accept cards.
  5. No tipping – Tipping is not expected and can be considered rude.
