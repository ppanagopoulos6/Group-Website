About Jordan

Jordan is a beautiful Middle Eastern country known for its rich history, stunning desert landscapes, and warm hospitality. It is home to Petra, one of the New Seven Wonders of the World, famous for its rock-cut architecture. Visitors can also float in the Dead Sea, the lowest point on Earth, explore the dramatic desert of Wadi Rum, and discover well-preserved Roman ruins in Jerash. Jordan offers a mix of ancient culture, natural wonders, delicious food like mansaf and falafel, and a safe, welcoming environment for tourists.

Amman

Amman, the capital of Jordan, is a vibrant city that combines ancient history with modern urban life, making it an excellent destination for tourists. Built on a series of hills, the city offers a mix of historic landmarks, cultural experiences, and lively neighborhoods. Visitors can explore the Amman Citadel to see ancient ruins and enjoy panoramic views of the city, or visit the impressive Roman Theatre in downtown Amman. Strolling along Rainbow Street allows tourists to experience local cafés, restaurants, shops, and a relaxed atmosphere. Food lovers can enjoy traditional Jordanian dishes such as mansaf, falafel, and hummus, while those interested in culture can visit museums like the Jordan Museum and browse local markets in the downtown souks. Amman is also a great base for day trips to nearby attractions such as Jerash, the Dead Sea, and Madaba, and in the evening, visitors can enjoy rooftop lounges, cafés, and a growing nightlife scene.

Sites you must visit

  • Petra

    Petra is Jordan’s most famous attraction and one of the world’s most impressive ancient cities. Often called the “Rose City” because of its pink sandstone cliffs, Petra was carved into the rock over 2,000 years ago. Visitors walk through the narrow Siq canyon before reaching the iconic Treasury, one of the most photographed landmarks in the world. Exploring Petra allows tourists to discover temples, tombs, and ancient streets, making it an unforgettable experience for history lovers and adventure seekers.

  • Wadi rum

    Wadi Rum is a stunning desert destination in southern Jordan, known for its vast red sands and dramatic rock formations. Nicknamed the “Valley of the Moon,” it offers visitors exciting activities such as jeep safaris, camel rides, hiking, and stargazing. Tourists can also stay overnight in traditional Bedouin camps to experience local culture and enjoy beautiful sunsets and star-filled skies, making Wadi Rum a perfect spot for adventure and nature lovers.

  • Jerash

    Jerash is one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world and a must-visit destination in Jordan. Tourists can walk through impressive ruins such as grand columns, ancient theaters, temples, and paved streets that date back over 2,000 years. Located north of Amman, Jerash offers visitors a chance to step back in time and experience Roman history in a beautiful and open setting, making it especially appealing for history and culture lovers.

The Dead-Sea

The Dead Sea is one of Jordan’s most unique natural attractions and a must-visit for tourists. Located at the lowest point on Earth, it is famous for its extremely salty water, which allows visitors to float effortlessly on the surface. The mineral-rich mud is known for its skin and health benefits, making it a popular spot for relaxation and wellness. Tourists can enjoy spa resorts, stunning desert and mountain views, and unforgettable sunsets, all while experiencing one of the world’s most extraordinary natural wonders.

Must taste

  • Mansaf – Jordan’s national dish, mansaf is tender lamb cooked in a yogurt sauce and served over rice, often enjoyed during celebrations.
  • Falafel – These crispy chickpea balls are a popular street food snack, packed with flavor and usually served with tahini or salad.
  • Hummus – A creamy blend of chickpeas, tahini, lemon, and olive oil, hummus is a staple appetizer or side dish in Jordanian meals.
  • Fresh Flatbread – Soft, warm, and often baked in clay ovens, fresh flatbread is served with almost every meal and perfect for scooping dips and sauces.
  • Maqluba – A traditional dish, maqluba is a layered rice, meat, and vegetables that is flipped upside down before serving, creating a flavorful dish.

!Tips!

  • Dress modestly – Especially in cities and religious sites; lightweight, covered clothing is ideal.
  • Carry cash – Small shops, taxis, and markets may not accept cards.
  • Stay hydrated – Jordan can be hot and dry, especially in the desert.
  • Use licensed taxis or ride apps – Agree on the fare or use the meter.
  • Plan desert visits carefully – Book guided tours for Wadi Rum and bring sun protection.
