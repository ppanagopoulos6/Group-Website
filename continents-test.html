The Americas (North & South)

In many parts of the world, "America" is taught as a single supercontinent. In English-speaking countries, it is usually divided into two:

  • North America: Includes the USA, Canada, and Mexico. It is economically wealthy and geographically diverse, ranging from the Arctic tundra in Northern Canada to the tropical beaches of the Caribbean.
  • South America: Home to the Amazon Rainforest (the "lungs of the Earth") and the Andes Mountains (the longest continental mountain range). It is rich in biodiversity and cultural history.
  • Key Fact: The Americas are the only landmasses that stretch nearly from pole to pole.
United States Canada Mexico Cuba Brazil

Africa: The Cradle of Humankind

Africa is the second-largest continent and is widely believed to be the birthplace of the human species (Homo sapiens). It contains 54 sovereign countries—the most of any continent.

  • Geography: Home to the world’s largest hot desert, the Sahara, and the world’s longest river, the Nile.
  • Nature: Africa hosts the planet's most iconic megafauna, including elephants, lions, giraffes, and rhinos.
  • Key Fact: It has the youngest population in the world, making it a region of significant future economic potential.
Egypt Kenya South Africa Nigeria Morocco

Europe: The Peninsula of Peninsulas

Physically, Europe is a large peninsula extending west from the supercontinent of Eurasia. Despite being the second-smallest continent, it has had a massive influence on global history.

  • Geography: Europe is defined by its irregular coastline and proximity to the sea. It features the Alps mountain range and the vast North European Plain.
  • Culture & Politics: Birthplace of Western civilization (via Ancient Greece and Rome) and the Industrial Revolution.
  • Key Fact: It is the third-most populous continent but faces an aging population compared to the rest of the world.
France Greece Spain Italy Germany

Asia: The Land of Extremes

Asia is the largest and most populous continent, covering nearly 30% of the Earth's land area and hosting roughly 60% of the human population.

  • Geography: It possesses the planet's most dramatic physical features, including the highest point on Earth (Mount Everest) and the lowest land point (The Dead Sea).
  • Culture & Economy: Asia is the birthplace of all the world's major religions and is currently the fastest-growing economic region.
  • Key Fact: It is home to more people than all other continents combined.
